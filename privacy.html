Privacy Policy for Reaction Bridge

Last Updated: 2025-09-03

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how the "Reaction Bridge" browser extension ("the App") handles your data. Our core principle is to respect your privacy and to collect, use, and store the minimum amount of data necessary to provide our service.

2. What Data We Access and Why

To function, the App uses the official Google YouTube Data API to perform actions on your behalf. We only access data when you explicitly initiate an action within the App's interface. The data we access includes:

  • YouTube Channel Information: We access your public YouTube channel name and thumbnail URL to display which account is currently active in the extension's popup.
  • Video and Channel IDs: We use the IDs of the original videos and channels to perform actions like liking, commenting, or subscribing.
  • Subscription Status: We check if you are already subscribed to a channel to display the correct state of the "Subscribe" button.
  • Video Rating Status: We check your "like" or "dislike" status on a video to display the correct state of the rating buttons.

3. How We Use Your Data

Your data is used exclusively to perform the core functions of the App, which you control directly:

  • To display your currently active YouTube channel.
  • To send your "like" or "dislike" action to the original video.
  • To post, edit, or delete your comments on the original video.
  • To subscribe or unsubscribe you from the original creator's channel.

4. Data Storage

We do not store any of your personal data on our own servers. The only piece of data the App stores is the ID of a comment you post through our interface. This data is stored locally and securely on your computer using the standard `chrome.storage.local` API. This allows you to manage (edit or delete) your comment later. This data never leaves your local device.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

Reaction Bridge does not share, sell, transfer, or disclose any of your personal or Google user data with any third parties. All API interactions happen directly between your browser and Google's servers.

6. Data Security

We take data security seriously. All communication with the Google YouTube Data API is performed over an encrypted HTTPS connection. Authentication is handled securely through Google's official OAuth2 protocol, meaning we never see or handle your password.

7. How to Revoke Access

You are in full control. You can revoke the App's access to your YouTube account at any time by visiting your Google Account permissions page and removing "Reaction Bridge" from the list of third-party apps with account access.

8. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us at: mjrduff@googlemail.com
